Securing Your First Custom Domain

A zero-jargon roadmap to claiming your digital street address and connecting it to the web in under 5 minutes.

STEP 1 Pick Your Domain Registrar

A domain registrar is the company you pay to claim ownership of your custom name. You don't buy domains forever; you rent them yearly (usually $10-$15/year).

Skip the expensive upsells and use one of these solid registrars:

1

Porkbun or Namecheap (Best for General Use)

Excellent pricing, zero sleazy hidden checkout fees, and free WHOIS privacy (which keeps spammers from harvesting your phone number and address).

2

Cloudflare Registrar (Cheapest / Developer Default)

They sell domains at cost ($0 markup!). If you plan to manage high-end DNS settings or vibe code complex projects, Cloudflare is the default choice.

STEP 2 Claiming Your Custom Name

Search for your desired domain. If possible, aim for a clean .com. They are the most memorable and authoritative.

If your exact name is taken, get creative:

• Add an active verb: use[brand].com or get[brand].com

• Add your niche: [brand]studio.com or [brand]labs.com

• Try modern tech extensions like .dev or .app if you are building toolkits.

STEP 3 Wiring Up the DNS (The Magic Part)

Buying the domain only secures the name. Connecting it to your hosting provider (like Vercel) requires configuring your DNS (Domain Name System) settings.

⚗️ The Alchemy Analogy

Think of your domain name as a custom business sign. The sign looks great, but right now it is sitting in your garage. Wiring up your DNS is like taking the sign out and nailing it to the actual street address where your store sits.

You only need to know two simple record types to connect any app:

A

A Records (The Street Address)

Maps a name directly to a server's numeric IP address. E.g., routing `digitalalchemy.dev` to Vercel's IP address `76.76.21.21`.

C

CNAME Records (The Forwarding Label)

Maps a sub-name to another name. E.g., telling `www.digitalalchemy.dev` to follow whatever the root domain is doing.
